20. Lookup History

This chapter validates per-book lookup history: recording, display, re-lookup, deletion, chain back-navigation, and the history cap.

Dictionary

english-full

Test A: Empty state

Open Lookup History from the reader menu before any lookups have been performed.

Steps

  1. Open the reader menu. Select Lookup History.
  2. The history screen opens and shows: No words looked up yet
  3. Press Back. Reader returns.

Test B: Direct lookup recorded

A direct hit must be recorded with the checkmark glyph.

Steps

  1. Look up apple (direct hit). Return to the reader.
  2. Open Lookup History.
  3. Verify apple appears at the top of the list with the checkmark glyph.

Test C: Not-found lookup recorded

A word with no match and no suggestions must be recorded with the X glyph.

Steps

  1. Look up zxqvmw (no match, no suggestions).
  2. Open Lookup History.
  3. Verify zxqvmw appears with the X glyph.

Test D: Stem lookup recorded

A word resolved via stemming must be recorded with the tilde glyph.

Steps

  1. Look up apples (resolved via stemming to apple).
  2. Open Lookup History.
  3. Verify the searched word appears with the tilde glyph.

Test E: Suggestion lookup recorded

A word resolved via the suggestions list must be recorded with the question mark glyph.

Steps

  1. Look up aplpe (misspelling). Select a suggestion from the list.
  2. Open Lookup History.
  3. Verify the selected suggestion word appears with the question mark glyph.

Test F: Re-lookup from history

Selecting a word from the history list must open its definition.

Steps

  1. Open Lookup History.
  2. Select a word and press Confirm (short press).
  3. Definition screen opens.
  4. Press Back. Returns to the history screen (not the reader).

Test G: Delete -- confirm

Long-pressing Confirm on a history entry must enter delete-confirm mode.

Steps

  1. Open Lookup History.
  2. Long-press Confirm on a history entry.
  3. A popup appears showing the entry word and Delete/Cancel button hints.
  4. Press Confirm (Delete).
  5. The entry is removed from the list.

Test H: Delete -- cancel

Cancelling delete-confirm mode must retain the entry.

Steps

  1. Open Lookup History.
  2. Long-press Confirm on a history entry.
  3. Press Back (Cancel).
  4. The entry is retained. The list returns to normal view.

Test I: Chain back-navigation

Chained lookups must support back-navigation through the chain.

Steps

  1. From the reader, look up word A. Definition screen appears.
  2. Enter word-select mode and look up word B. B's definition appears.
  3. Press Back from B. A's definition re-appears (chain back).
  4. Press Back again. Returns to word-select or reader (not B's definition).
  5. Open Lookup History. Verify both A and B entries are present.

Test J: History cap (eviction)

The history list must respect the configured cap, evicting the oldest entries.

Steps

  1. Set the Dictionary History Limit setting to 25.
  2. Perform 26 distinct lookups.
  3. Open Lookup History.
  4. Verify only 25 entries are shown. The oldest (first) lookup has been evicted.

Test K: Done exits to reader

Long-pressing Back from a chained definition must exit directly to the reader.

Steps

  1. Look up word A. From A's definition, enter word-select and look up word B.
  2. Long-press Back (Done).
  3. Exits directly to the reader, bypassing the history screen and word-select mode.

Test L: List navigation -- wrap-around and paging

The history list must support wrap-around single-step navigation and page-at-a-time navigation.

Note

Ensure the history list contains at least two full pages of entries (more entries than fit on one screen). If needed, look up enough distinct words to fill two pages before opening Lookup History.

Steps

  1. Open Lookup History. The cursor is on the first (most recent) entry.
  2. Press Previous once. The cursor wraps to the last (oldest) entry.
  3. Press Next once. The cursor wraps back to the first entry.
  4. Hold Next (continuous press). The cursor jumps forward one page at a time.
  5. Continue paging past the last entry. The cursor wraps to the beginning.
  6. Hold Previous (continuous press). The cursor pages backward, wrapping from first to last.
